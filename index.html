Contextual fit · Rome as an economic system

Why Rome? Because finance is also a question of systems.

I did not choose Rome for its monuments. I chose it because Rome offers a rare sequence: an ancient lesson in scale, a contemporary concentration of financial institutions, and a modern research campus built for focused analytical work.

Rome as an early architecture of globalisation

I do not read ancient Rome as a museum. I read it as an early attempt to coordinate scale: roads, ports, aqueducts, law, coinage and logistics moving people, goods and authority across a vast economic space.

For a Finance and Banking candidate, the parallel is direct. A monetary system is not only money; it is credibility, circulation, enforcement, infrastructure and trust.

Traffic-based profitability model for OOH/DOOH advertising in Casablanca

Executive Summary

This project asked a concrete question: which advertising locations in Casablanca are financially attractive once traffic, area context, regulation and cost are considered together?

Problem

Outdoor advertising is often chosen by instinct: “this place looks visible.” I tested a stricter route using traffic simulation, GIS mapping and financial modelling.

Data / Inputs

  • SUMO traffic simulation outputs
  • GIS / spatial mapping
  • Socio-economic and demographic indicators
  • Regulatory constraints
  • CAPEX
  • OPEX
  • TCO
  • ROI assumptions

Methodology

  1. Model traffic flows with SUMO.
  2. Map zones and corridors with GIS.
  3. Add socio-economic and regulatory criteria.
  4. Build CAPEX/OPEX/TCO scenarios.
  5. Calculate ROI and break-even logic.
  6. Construct IRP ranking.

Tools

SUMO, QGIS/GIS mapping, Excel financial modelling, CAPEX/OPEX/TCO scenario logic, ROI and break-even analysis.

Outputs

Potential Profitability Index, five-year scenarios, location scoring, traffic-exposure maps and a simulation dashboard.

Results

Scenario-based estimate: The strongest scenario produced a projected maximum ROI of 533% and a 5-week break-even point under the stated assumptions.

Limitations

These are scenarios, not field results. A real deployment would need updated costs, occupancy data, local permissions and on-site validation.

What this proves about me

This shows how I connect mobility data, spatial analysis and finance without hiding the assumptions behind the score.

Case study

AI-MCDM investment scorecard for smart-city infrastructure

A ranking model for infrastructure choices under carbon, energy and geopolitical uncertainty.

Executive Summary

This research built a ranking model for smart-city infrastructure projects exposed to carbon, energy and geopolitical uncertainty.

Problem

A simple NPV view can miss too much: carbon prices, energy volatility, geopolitical risk and public-value criteria.

Data / Inputs

  • World Bank WDI / ESG indicators
  • World Bank PPI database
  • EIB project and pipeline records
  • EIBIS indicators
  • Carbon-price scenarios
  • GPR index
  • Constructed 25-project scoring matrix

Methodology

  1. XGBoost demand forecasting
  2. Ridge benchmark comparison
  3. Carbon-adjusted DNPV
  4. Real Options valuation
  5. Fuzzy-AHP / MEREC weighting
  6. AROMAN ranking
  7. Sensitivity analysis
  8. Cross-method comparison

Tools

Python workflow, XGBoost, Ridge regression, DNPV, Real Options, Fuzzy-AHP, MEREC, AROMAN, TOPSIS/COPRAS comparison, sensitivity analysis.

Outputs

A 25-project ranking, validation outputs, sensitivity checks, traceability notes and replication-package logic.

Results

  • Model-validation result: 45.8% lower MAE versus Ridge in original temporal validation.
  • Self-developed model: 25-project ranking framework.
  • Exploratory research output: Carbon-price threshold effect.
  • Rankings diverged from NPV-only ranking.
  • Framework supports auditability, traceability and sensitivity disclosure.

Limitations

The model supports judgment; it does not replace it. Some tests remained exploratory, and H3 was not confirmed at full panel level.

What this proves about me

This shows that I can use AI-assisted modelling for investment appraisal while keeping uncertainty visible.
